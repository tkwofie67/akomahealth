Prevention Tips
Evidence-based · GHS & WHO guidelines
Malaria Prevention
Sleep under an insecticide-treated bednet (ITN) every night — especially children under 5 and pregnant women
Remove standing water — old tyres, pots, gutters — where mosquitoes breed near your home
Use indoor residual spraying (IRS) when offered by your district health team
Pregnant women: take IPTp-SP at every ANC visit from the second trimester onwards
Children 5–17 months: malaria vaccine (R21/Matrix-M) now available — ask your CHPS compound
Maternal Health
Attend at least 8 ANC visits during pregnancy — the first before 12 weeks
Take iron and folic acid tablets daily throughout pregnancy
Deliver at a health facility with a skilled birth attendant — not at home alone
Breastfeed exclusively for the first 6 months — protects babies from infection
Child Health
Follow the Ghana Immunisation Schedule — vaccines protect against measles, polio, meningitis and more
Wash hands with soap before feeding children and after using the toilet
Weigh your child monthly at the CHPS compound to monitor growth and catch malnutrition early
